Current Appointment

 

Associate professor, Harry S Truman School of Public Affairs. August 2000 to present.  Administer two National Science Foundation cooperative agreements: Missouri Alliance for Graduate Education and the Professorate (MAGEP) and the Louis Stokes Missouri Alliance for Minority Participation (LS MoAMP) as .50fte duties during AY 2000/2001. MAGEP is a five year agreement in its third year which includes each of the University of Missouri campuses that offer doctoral degrees in life and physical sciences including engineering and mathematics.  The MAGEP project is supported by $3M from the Foundation and provides support for 32 doctoral students on University of Missouri campuses. LS MoAMP is an undergraduate project which includes all public institutions that offer baccalaureate programs in life and physical sciences and both the community college systems in St Louis and Kansas City, Missouri. State agencies including the Department of Conservation, Department of Natural Resources, the Coordinating Board of Higher Education (CBHE) and the Department of Elementary and Secondary Education (DESE) are also vital partners in the consortium.  The LS MoAMP has been supported by $10M from the NSF and alliance members.  The partnership facilitated an increase of 86% in baccalaureate degrees over the baseline in 1995.

 

My research agenda focuses on American municipalities, their revenue streams, expenditures decisions, governance processes and administrative structures. The research is closely tied to the examination of public policy on voting rights. This entails the exploration of the impact of federalism and/or intergovernmental fission on subsequent voting rights policies, policy implementation and policy outcomes.  The venture has lead to creation of a database containing every municipality in the US which elected mayors subsequent to passage of the 1965 voting rights act ands subsequent amendments.  Decennial census and census of government data collected since 1960 provide the source for more than 75 variables which describe population make-up, population size, municipal revenue sources and related variables and constructs.
